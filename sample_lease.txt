RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT (SAMPLE)

1. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

1.1 Landlord Responsibility
The Landlord shall be responsible for all major structural repairs, including but not limited to the roof, foundation, and exterior walls.
The Landlord is responsible for maintaining all provided appliances (refrigerator, stove, dishwasher) in working order, unless damage is caused by Tenant misuse.
All plumbing issues related to main lines, recurring leaks not caused by Tenant, and water heater failures are the Landlord's responsibility.
The Landlord is responsible for tree trimming and major landscaping work.

1.2 Tenant Responsibility
The Tenant shall keep the premises in a clean and sanitary condition.
The Tenant is responsible for replacing all consumable items, including but not limited to lightbulbs, smoke detector batteries, and HVAC filters (every 3 months).
Minor plumbing issues caused by Tenant negligence (e.g., clogged toilets due to foreign objects) are the Tenant's responsibility.
The Tenant is responsible for general lawn care, including mowing and watering.

2. EMERGENCIES

2.1 Definition
An emergency is defined as any situation that poses an immediate threat to the health and safety of the occupants or significant damage to the property. Examples include fire, flooding, gas leaks, or loss of heat during winter months.

2.2 Procedure
In case of a life-threatening emergency (fire, medical), call 911 immediately.
For property emergencies (burst pipe, gas leak), contact the Emergency Property Manager Line at 555-0199 after ensuring safety.

3. UTILITIES
The Tenant is responsible for all utilities including electricity, water, gas, and internet, unless otherwise specified in the addendum.

4. QUIET ENJOYMENT
Tenant shall not disturb the quiet enjoyment of other tenants or neighbors. Quiet hours are from 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM daily.
